PORTION TRACKING — "Know Exactly How Many Portions Are Available"
================================================================================

Label: Portion Tracking
Heading: Know Exactly How Many Portions Are Available
Subtitle: RecipeBuilder tracks the current portion count for every recipe in your system. Whether you produce 50 servings of a salad or 200 units of a packaged snack, the stock number is always visible and always current. No separate inventory software, no paper logs — just a number you can trust.

How Real-Time Tracking Works:

1. Each recipe has a stock count — the number of portions currently available. This count lives directly on the recipe card in your dashboard, so you never have to navigate away to check inventory.

2. Update with quick controls — tap -10, -5, -1, +1, +5, or +10 to adjust the count instantly. Sold five portions? Tap -5. Produced ten more? Tap +10. The interface is designed for speed, not data entry.

3. Set stock to an exact value — after a physical count or a fresh production batch, type the exact number and confirm. The stock resets to your specified value immediately.

4. Timestamp on every update — RecipeBuilder records when the stock was last modified. At a glance, managers can see which recipes have fresh counts and which may need verification.

QUICK CONTROLS — "Update Stock in Seconds, Not Minutes"
================================================================================

Label: Quick Controls
Heading: Update Stock in Seconds, Not Minutes
Subtitle: Kitchen environments are fast-paced. RecipeBuilder gives your team dedicated stock adjustment controls so they can update portion counts without interrupting their workflow. Every control is one tap — no forms, no confirmation dialogs, no friction.

1. Quick Stock Update Controls
   Adjust stock counts instantly with dedicated controls: -10, -5, -1, +1, +5, +10. No typing required. Kitchen staff can update portion counts in seconds between orders, during shift changes, or after a production run.

2. Set Stock to a Specific Value
   Need to set stock to an exact number after a physical count or a new production batch? Enter the value directly and the stock count updates immediately. Ideal for end-of-day reconciliation or restocking after a large prep session.

3. Last Updated Timestamp
   Every stock update is timestamped so you always know when the count was last changed. This gives managers visibility into how recently inventory was checked and helps identify recipes that may need a fresh count.

4. Stock Count on Recipe Dashboard
   The current stock count for each recipe is visible directly on the recipe dashboard. No need to open a separate inventory screen — you see at a glance how many portions are available alongside every other recipe detail.

UNIFIED DASHBOARD — "Everything About a Recipe in One View"
================================================================================

Label: Unified Dashboard
Heading: Everything About a Recipe in One View
Subtitle: Stock count is just one piece of the picture. RecipeBuilder displays prep time, per serving details, selling price, and barcode alongside inventory — so your team has all the information they need without switching between screens or systems.

1. Prep Time
   Each recipe displays its prep time so your team knows how long production takes. When stock runs low, you can immediately see whether a recipe can be replenished within your service window or if you need to plan ahead.

2. Per Serving Information
   Serving size in grams and per serving cost in AED are displayed alongside the stock count. This means you can make restocking decisions with full awareness of portion economics — how much each serving weighs and what it costs.

3. Barcode at a Glance
   Every recipe carries a unique barcode displayed on the dashboard. Use it for product identification, retail scanning, or point-of-sale integration. The barcode is always visible — no need to generate or look it up separately.

4. Production Scheduling Support
   Plan your production runs based on current stock levels and anticipated demand. When you can see exactly how many portions remain for every recipe, scheduling the next production batch becomes a straightforward decision rather than a guess.

Heading: Plan Production Runs With Confidence

Data-Driven Production Decisions:
When you can see exactly how many portions of each recipe remain, deciding what to produce next becomes straightforward.
