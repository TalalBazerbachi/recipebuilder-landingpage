HOW IT WORKS — "From Recipe to Label in Minutes"
================================================================================

Section Badge: How It Works
Section Heading: From Recipe to Label in Minutes
Subtitle: Three simple steps to create compliant food labels from your recipes.

This section presents the core 3-step workflow that shows how RecipeBuilder transforms a raw recipe into a finished, regulation-ready food label. It is designed as a dark-background section with a subtle grid pattern and two blurred gradient orbs for visual depth. Each step is displayed as a card in a 3-column grid (stacked on mobile), with connector lines between them on desktop to indicate the sequential flow.

Step 01 — Create Your Recipe
Add ingredients from the USDA database, set quantities and units, define preparation methods, calculate costs, and upload cover photos.

What happens here: The user starts by entering their recipe into RecipeBuilder. They search the built-in USDA FoodData Central database (20,000+ verified ingredients), select ingredients, and specify exact quantities and units. They can also define how the recipe is prepared (cooking instructions, temperatures, etc.), assign ingredient costs for financial tracking, and upload a cover photo for easy visual identification. This step captures all the raw data that RecipeBuilder needs to perform automatic calculations in the next step.

Step 02 — Auto-Generate Labels
Nutrition facts, packaging artwork, barcodes, and QR codes are generated automatically. Labels support multi-language translation.

What happens here: Once the recipe is saved with its ingredients and quantities, RecipeBuilder automatically calculates the complete nutrition profile — calories, macronutrients (protein, carbs, fat), micronutrients (vitamins, minerals), and more — all based on USDA data. From this nutrition data, the platform generates several outputs simultaneously:
- An FDA-style Nutrition Facts panel with per-serving values, % Daily Values, and proper formatting
- Full packaging artwork that includes the nutrition panel, a bilingual ingredient list, allergen declarations, storage instructions, and manufacturer details
- A unique barcode for product identification and retail scanning
- A QR code that links to a live nutrition facts page consumers can scan
- Traffic light labels (green/amber/red indicators for fat, sugars, salt) for consumer clarity
Labels can be translated into 40+ languages automatically, making them ready for international markets.

Step 03 — Export & Manage
Download labels as PNG or PDF. Track inventory with real-time stock counts, monitor ingredient costs and profit margins.

What happens here: The finished labels are available for immediate download in print-ready formats (high-resolution PNG for digital use, PDF for professional printing). Beyond label generation, RecipeBuilder also serves as an ongoing operational tool — users can track how many portions of each recipe are in stock with real-time inventory counts, monitor the cost of every ingredient to calculate per-serving cost and profit margins, and manage their entire recipe catalog from a single dashboard. This step represents the transition from label creation to day-to-day business management.

Visual Details:
- Each step has a large icon container with a gradient background that subtly rotates on hover
- Step numbers (01, 02, 03) appear as gradient badges on each card
- Horizontal connector lines run between the steps on desktop to show the flow
- The entire section animates in with staggered fade-up effects when scrolled into view

A: Minutes. Create your recipe, add ingredients from the USDA database, and RecipeBuilder auto-generates your nutrition label, packaging artwork, barcode, and QR code instantly. Most users have their first label ready within 10 minutes of signing up.
